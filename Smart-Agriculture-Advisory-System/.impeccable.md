## Design Context

### Users
Indian farmers and extension officers in Tamil Nadu (Kanchipuram district). They use this system on laptops/desktops at district agricultural offices or on personal devices. Their job: monitor farm conditions, receive weather-based advisories, and make timely crop management decisions. Many users have moderate tech literacy — the interface must be instantly clear and actionable.

### Brand Personality
**Trustworthy, grounded, warm.** This is a government/research-grade agricultural tool, not a consumer app. It should feel reliable and professional but approachable — like a knowledgeable agronomist sitting beside you. No cold corporate or flashy startup energy.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Warm, earthy, professional — inspired by agricultural dashboards and Indian government portals done right. Think terracotta, deep soil browns, muted leaf greens, harvest gold accents on a warm cream background.
- **Light mode primary** (farmers work outdoors, need readability)
- **NOT**: Generic green SaaS dashboard, cyan-on-dark tech aesthetic, glassmorphism
- **Typography**: Clean, legible, warm — nothing cold or monospace
- **Layout**: Information-dense but well-organized — farmers need data at a glance, not empty space

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every element must be instantly understandable. No ambiguous icons, no hidden features.
2. **Data density with breathing room** — Show the information farmers need without overwhelming them. Use hierarchy, not hiding.
3. **Grounded warmth** — Colors, textures, and tone should feel connected to the earth and agriculture, not generic tech.
4. **Actionable first** — Every advisory, alert, and data point should lead to a clear next step.
5. **Respectful of context** — This serves real farmers making real decisions. The design must earn their trust through consistency and reliability.
